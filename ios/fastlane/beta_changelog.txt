Added — Native Apple Health sync is live
• The app now reads your recovery data (sleep stages, HRV, resting HR, respiratory rate, wrist temperature, body mass, VO₂ max) straight from Apple Health and sends it to Iron Trainer — no more Health Auto Export needed. It syncs when you open the app, hourly in the background, and on demand.

Test on device
1. Settings → "Apple Health" → if you haven't already, tap "Connect Apple Health" and grant the 7 metrics.
2. Tap "Sync now" → the button shows "Syncing…" then returns; "Last native sync" should update to "just now". No "Server stored no data" error (if you see that, your sign-in expired — re-pair in Settings).
3. Give it a moment, then check the Dashboard / Recovery tab (or the web app) — your latest sleep, HRV and resting-HR should be present and match what Apple Health shows.
4. Background: leave the app, wait, reopen later — "Last native sync" should have advanced on its own (foreground catch-up), and ideally between opens (hourly background delivery).
5. Sanity: the numbers should match your existing Health Auto Export data (both run in parallel during migration — keep HAE set up for now).

Notes
• HRV needs an Apple Watch — Garmin doesn't write HRV to Apple Health, so it may stay blank if you only wear a Garmin.
• The old "Health Auto Export (legacy)" section still works and stays for now; we'll retire it once native sync is confirmed on your device.
• Also still testable from the previous build: the Readiness widget (Home / Lock Screen / StandBy).
